# Marketing Handoff: Pulse Sprint 1

## What is newly marketable

- Executive cyber scorecards generated from live operational signals, not static spreadsheets.
- A living cyber risk register tied to TrustOps findings, evidence gaps, tasks, and assessment pressure.
- A 30 / 60 / 90 remediation roadmap that leadership can review and track.
- Durable board brief and quarterly review workflows inside the product.
- Command Center roll-up from operator activity to executive posture.

## Screenshots and workflows the public site should show

- Pulse dashboard with current posture score, delta, open top risks, and overdue roadmap items.
- Risk Register view showing sourced risks, severity, owner, due date, and filters.
- Roadmap view showing 30 / 60 / 90 items with owners and expected impact.
- Board Brief view with top risks, overdue actions, leadership decisions needed, and review-gated export controls.
- Quarterly Review view showing recurring leadership cadence, decisions, and follow-up actions.
- Command Center Pulse layer linking TrustOps operations to executive visibility.

## Suggested messaging angles

- "Turn trust work into executive visibility."
- "Give leadership one clear cyber posture view."
- "Move from questionnaire firefighting to recurring board-level readiness."
- "Convert findings, gaps, and remediation work into an owned 30 / 60 / 90 plan."
- "Make cyber posture a recurring operating rhythm, not a point-in-time audit scramble."

## Proof points and CTA suggestions

Proof points:

- scorecards are based on real product signals such as findings, overdue work, TrustOps backlog, and assessments
- risk register is durable and editable, not a transient AI output
- board brief export is review-gated and tied to current posture and roadmap
- quarterly review cadence persists decisions and follow-up actions

Suggested CTAs:

- `Open Pulse`
- `Generate Executive Scorecard`
- `See The Risk Register`
- `Draft Board Brief`
- `Book a TrustOps + Pulse Demo`

## How Pulse improves module attach and recurring value

- Pulse gives executive buyers a reason to stay engaged after trust-response work is complete.
- TrustOps becomes more valuable because its outputs now roll into recurring posture and board reporting.
- The combination supports multi-module contracts: TrustOps for procurement readiness and Pulse for executive cadence.
- Pulse creates weekly and quarterly return paths, which is stronger for retention than audit-only workflows.
