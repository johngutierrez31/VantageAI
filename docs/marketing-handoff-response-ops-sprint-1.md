# Marketing Handoff: Response Ops Sprint 1

## New marketable capabilities

- guided first-hour incident triage inside the same system used for trust, posture, and AI governance
- incident-linked runbook task packs with ownership and decision trail
- review-gated after-action reporting with executive-ready exports
- lightweight tabletop workflows that convert gaps into findings, risks, and tasks
- executive carry-over of active incidents and post-incident work into Pulse and Command Center

## Screenshots and workflows to showcase

- Response Ops dashboard showing active incidents, overdue incident actions, post-incident actions, and tabletop cadence
- incident detail showing timeline, runbook packs, linked findings and risks, and after-action review panel
- approved after-action export flow
- tabletop detail showing decisions, gaps, follow-up tasks, findings, and risks
- Command Center or Pulse carry-over card showing incident pressure influencing executive posture

## Suggested messaging angles

- "From governance to day-of-incident execution"
- "Run first-hour triage, owned response tasks, and incident closeout in one operating system"
- "Turn incidents and tabletop lessons into board-visible remediation"
- "Give lean teams a practical incident command layer without buying a heavyweight SOAR platform"

## Proof points and CTA suggestions

- proof point: incident creation launches a structured first-hour workflow instead of blank notes
- proof point: runbook packs and timeline entries are durable records tied to the incident
- proof point: after-action reports are review-gated and exportable
- proof point: tabletop gaps become findings, risks, and tasks automatically
- CTA: "Start Incident Triage"
- CTA: "Open Response Ops"
- CTA: "See how Pulse reflects live incident pressure"

## How Response Ops improves module attach and premium value

- makes the platform operationally credible beyond trust and reporting
- creates a clear premium upsell for teams that need incident readiness and managed review support
- increases recurring usage because incidents, follow-up tasks, and exercises drive weekly operator return
- reinforces TrustOps, Pulse, and AI Governance by feeding incidents back into the shared posture and remediation model
