# Board Briefs and Quarterly Reviews

## Board Briefs

### What they are for

Board Briefs are concise executive-ready reporting artifacts generated from Pulse posture, top risks, and roadmap state.

### How to use them

1. Generate the board brief from current Pulse records
2. Review the overall posture summary
3. Confirm the top risks are framed in business terms
4. Make sure overdue actions and decisions needed are explicit
5. Approve before export

### Best use cases

- board updates
- executive leadership briefings
- recurring cyber summaries

## Quarterly Reviews

### What they are for

Quarterly Reviews are the durable record of the actual leadership review cycle.

### How to use them

1. Prepare the quarterly review from the current snapshot, board brief, risks, and roadmap
2. Record participants and notes
3. Capture decisions made
4. Capture follow-up actions
5. Finalize the review as the system of record

### Best use cases

- recurring security governance reviews
- operating committee meetings
- quarter-close security reporting

## Operator tip

A board brief tells leadership what matters. A quarterly review records what leadership decided to do about it.
